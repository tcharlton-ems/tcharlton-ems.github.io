    Asthma - COPD

    ALL

    I. Inclusion Criteria

    A. Patient’s age is 16 years or older.

    B. The patient has a history of asthma, emphysema or COPD AND complains of a worsening shortness of breath.

    C. Lung exam has wheezing, rales/rhonchi, or poor air exchange.

    Medic

    D. EKG Findings indicate normal sinus rhythm, sinus tachycardia or atrial fibrillation with controlled ventricular response. If other rhythm is present, then proceed to the appropriate arrhythmia protocol.

    EMT

    A. Protocol

    1. If available, request ALS back-up for:

    a. Pediatric patient, who is wheezing, grunting, has retractions, stridor, or any other signs of respiratory distress.

    b. Patient who doesn’t have a prescribed inhaler and the transport time is greater than 30 minutes.

    1. Check to see if the patient has already taken any inhaler or nebulizer doses prior to arrival. Note time and amount.

    2. Do not use the inhaler if any of the following are present:

    a. Inability of patient to use device.

    b. Inhaler is not prescribed for the patient.

    c. Medication is expired.

    d. If the patient has met the maximum prescribed dose of their inhaler according to prescription label, contact medical control.

    1. Administer Albuterol (Proventil) aerosol 2.5mg/2.5ml via nebulizer. Consider adding 1 vial Ipratropium Bromide (0.5mg of 0.017%) to the Albuterol aerosol. May substitute Duoneb (Albuterol plus Ipratropium Bromide that is premixed) for all Albuterol treatments.

    a. OR Assist the patient with using their own metered-dose inhaler if nebulizer is not available.

    1. To assist with administration of a metered-dose inhaler:

    a. Make sure inhaler is at room temperature and shake several times to mix the medication.

    b. Take oxygen mask off the patient.

    c. Tell the patient to exhale deeply and put the mouthpiece in front of the mouth. If the patient has a spacer device, it should be used.

    d. Have patient depress the metered-dose inhaler as they begin to inhale deeply.

    e. Instruct the patient to hold their breath for as long as comfortable, so the medication can be absorbed.

    f. Put oxygen mask back on the patient.

    g. Repeat a dose after one minute. If further medication is necessary beyond the patient’s prescribed number of doses, contact medical control.

    1. Recheck vital signs (including pulse oximetry if available) and perform focused assessment.

    Medic

    1. If the patient is in impending respiratory failure, obtain IV access.

    2. If multiple Albuterol treatments are anticipated, administer Prednisone 60 mg PO or Solu-Medrol (Methylprednisolone) 60 mg IV or PO.

    3. If signs of impending respiratory failure (see notes):

    A. Consider initiating non-invasive positive pressure ventilation (BIPAP or CPAP). Start at 5 cmH2O and titrate higher as tolerated by patient.

    B. ASTHMA ONLY: Consider administering epinephrine 0.3 mg IM (1mg/ml) followed by magnesium sulfate 2 g IV/IO diluted in 100 ml normal saline over 20 minutes.

    1. Consider repetitive Albuterol treatments if needed, up to a total of three treatments.

    ALL

    B. Consider PAP, reference protocol T709.

    Notes:

    A. When attempting to differentiate between COPD and congestive heart failure, the medication history will usually give more valuable information than will the physical exam.

    B. Ipratropium Bromide is an anticholinergic medication and may cause tachycardia. Do not use on patients with narrow angle glaucoma or patients with bladder neck obstruction (history of urinary retention).

    C. There is growing evidence that steroids (Prednisone or Solu-Medrol (Methylprednisolone) for adults may be beneficial.

    D. Solu-Medrol (Methyprednisolone) can be given orally to adult patients, though the IV route is preferred.

    E. Signs of impending respiratory failure

    1. Depressed mental status or excessive sleepiness

    2. Agitation, panic, or sensation of drowning

    3. Inability to maintain respiratory effort.

    4. Cyanosis or worsening hypoxia
