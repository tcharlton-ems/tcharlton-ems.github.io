<h1 id="superficial-tranexamic-acid-txa-administration">Superficial Tranexamic Acid (TXA) Administration</h1>
<h2 id="all">ALL</h2>
<p><strong>I. INCLUSION CRITERIA</strong></p>
<p>A. Any age</p>
<p>B. Superficial bleeding injury without hemostasis from pressure alone, such as:</p>
<ol>
<li>
<p>Anterior epistaxis not hemostatic after pinching nose while leaning forward for 10 minutes</p>
</li>
<li>
<p>Superficial skin injury not hemostatic after 10 minutes of pressure</p>
</li>
</ol>
<p>C. Non-profuse hemorrhage where application of direct pressure isn’t ideal (ex. penile lacerations)</p>
<p><strong>II. EXCLUSION CRITERIA</strong></p>
<p>A. Previous allergic reaction to TXA.</p>
<p>B. Patients with clear contraindications for anti-fibrinolytic agents (evidence of active intravascular thrombotic disease such as PE or DVT or disseminated intravascular coagulation).</p>
<p>C. Deep or penetrating injury(ies) (e.g. GSW, tracking wounds, wounds with pulsatile/arterial bleeding).</p>
<ol>
<li>For these injuries, follow currently established protocols.</li>
</ol>
<p>D. Preceding administration of IV TXA.</p>
<p>E. Not for use with gauze prior to being used as packing into wounds</p>
<p>F. Bleeding from a dialysis fistula.</p>
<p><strong>III. PROTOCOL</strong></p>
<p>A. Secure airway following T705 Airway Procedure.</p>
<p>B. Control all other bleeding sources and manage any hemorrhagic shock.</p>
<p>C. Obtain vitals</p>
<p>D. Transport patient to appropriate facility</p>
<p>E. Notify receiving hospital with patient information as soon as possible</p>
<h2 id="medic">Medic</h2>
<p>F. After ensuring patient meets all inclusion criteria:</p>
<ol>
<li>For superficial wounds:</li>
</ol>
<p>a. Soak gauze in the undiluted contents (100 mg/mL) of a standard ampule of tranexamic acid solution used for injection.</p>
<p>b. Place TXA soaked gauze over wound and apply pressure for 10 minutes.</p>
<p>c. After 10 minutes of applied pressure, if not yet at receiving hospital, wrap wound with dressing leaving TXA soaked gauze in place over wound.</p>
<ol start="2">
<li>For Epistaxis Refer to Epistaxis protocol S508.</li>
</ol>
